Building-Integrated Solar Air Heating Systems Hit Milestone

April 18, 2014

WASHINGTON, DC – According to data released today by the Solar Energy Industries Association (SEIA), a growing sector of the industry has reached a major milestone, with 5 million square feet of building-integrated solar air heating collectors now installed in North America.

SEIA President and CEO Rhone Resch says these systems represent 250 megawatts (MW) of thermal energy and displace nearly 100,000 tons of CO2 each year from the atmosphere.

Major International Labor Union Joins SAHWIA to Help Promote Solar Air Heating

September 17, 2013

September 16, 2013: As solar continues to be a "hot" growth industry in the United States and other parts of the world, the International Association of Sheet Metal, Air, Rail and Transportation Workers (SMART) is feeling especially buoyant about the growth potential for its members from increased use of solar air heating. To lend support to the industry, they have become members of the Solar Air Heating World Industries Association (SAHWIA) and will provide assistance to help promote the use of solar air heating, which will create installation work for their members.

Solar Air Heating World Industries Association Appoints Executive Director

July 27, 2012

Madrid, Spain: July 27, 2012 – The Solar Air Heating World Industries Association (SAHWIA) is pleased to announce the appointment of Cecile Tabarot to the position of Executive Director effective June 1st. Ms. Tabarot will direct SAHWIA's efforts from the new European office in Madrid, Spain.

Canada: Solar Air Heating is Strongest Sector

November 15, 2011

2010 was a good year for the solar thermal industry in Canada. The total newly installed collector area grew by 54%, from 129,418 to 199,491 m2. Air collector sales doubled and — for the first time — overtook the solar swimming pool market, which is more or less stagnating. With 150%, the segment of glazed solar water collectors experienced the highest growth, although the 20,000 m2 sold in 2010 makes it still a low-level market.

Canada Releases World's First Test Standard for Unglazed Ambient Solar Air Collectors

October 7, 2011

Canadian Standards Association released in September 2011 an updated edition of the CSA F378 Series, Solar Collectors, which replaces the previous edition of CSA F378, Solar Collectors, published in 1987.

The new test standard currently has two sections:

  • F378.1, Glazed and unglazed liquid heating solar collectors
  • F378.2, Air heating solar collectors

Europe Will Not Meet 20% Renewable Energy Target by 2020

July 27, 2011

Europe will not be able to meet its obligation to supply 20% of its energy from renewable energy by 2020, states John Hollick, CEO of SolarWall Europe, Sarl. and Chairman of the Solar Air Heating World Industries Association (SAHWIA), due to an error in definition made by the European Parliament.

Bombardier Installs Solar Air Heating System as Part of Green Mission

June 29, 2011

Wednesday, June 29, 2011 – Bombardier Inc. has been a corporate role model of energy efficiency for years, but recently the world's third largest aircraft manufacturer took initial steps toward more green technology by using a solar energy retrofit to preheat outdoor air at its Mirabel, Quebec industrial building.

Toyota Motor Manufacturing France Installs SolarWall System

June 22, 2011

Valenciennes, France: June 22, 2011 — Toyota Motor Manufacturing France (TMMF) has inaugurated a SolarWall® installation at its facility in Valenciennes, France which produces the Yaris (Vitz) for Europe. The SolarWall® technology is a solar air heating system that uses the sun's energy to heat ventilation air for commercial and industrial buildings. It substantially reduces traditional heating fuel expenses and GHG emissions. This technology, developed by Conserval Engineering, will help TMMF with their commitment to deploy renewable energy to reduce CO2 emissions.

CA Group to Install Europe's Largest SolarWall System

June 2, 2011

June 2, 2011 – Leading metal roofing and cladding system manufacturer and installer, CA Group, has been awarded the contract to supply and install the latest 80,000 m2 distribution centre for a high street retail giant, in Castle Donnington. The development will incorporate Europe's largest Transpired Solar Collector, or SolarWall®, installation on a single building. SolarWall® is widely recognised as one of the most efficient and cost effective renewable systems available.
