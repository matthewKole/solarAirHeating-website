The Technology

What Is Solar Air Heating?

Solar air heating uses wall- or roof-mounted collector panels to capture the sun's energy and preheat the fresh air drawn into a building's ventilation system — before that air ever reaches your furnace or HVAC unit.

Unlike solar water systems, no pipes, pumps, or heat exchangers are needed. The air itself is the working fluid, making solar air collectors one of the simplest, most durable forms of solar thermal technology available.

Commercial warehouses, agricultural buildings, airports, and residences around the world use solar air heating to dramatically cut their energy bills — especially in climates with cold winters and abundant winter sunshine.
